The problem

Remote coaching breaks at scale.

Past 8 athletes, the spreadsheet stops working. You spend Sundays scrolling Strava feeds, rebuilding context you already had two weeks ago, and the athlete who needs you most gets noticed last.

01 / SCATTERED

Data lives in seven tabs.

Strava, TrainingPeaks, WhatsApp, Google Sheets, Garmin Connect, your notebook, your memory. Nothing reconciles. By the time you've gathered context, the week is over.

Avg. tools per coach 7apps
02 / MANUAL

Analysis eats the weekend.

You open each athlete's week, compare planned vs. actual, eyeball the load, type a note. Multiply by 20. The expert work — the call you'd make in a second — gets buried under file management.

Hours / week on review 11hrs
03 / REACTIVE

You catch problems late.

Overtraining, missed sessions, declining HRV — the signals are there. But by the time they surface in a Sunday review, you're absorbing damage instead of preventing it. Coaches react when they should be steering.

Issues caught after the fact 63%

How it works

Three steps. Then you coach.

Setup is honest: connect Strava, write your plan, let the system match what's planned to what actually happened. You stay in your craft. The work that doesn't need a coach gets handled before it reaches you.

01

Athlete connects Strava.

One link. Activities sync automatically — pace, power, HR, GPS, sleep if connected. No CSV exports, no manual logs. The athlete uses the apps they already use.

↻
02

Coach writes the plan.

Drag sessions onto the calendar, set zones, attach intent notes. Reuse blocks across athletes. The plan is yours — the assistant never writes it for you.

PL
03

AI flags what matters.

Every morning: who hit it, who missed, who's drifting. Suggested adjustments arrive with citations and one-click approval. Nothing changes until you say so.
